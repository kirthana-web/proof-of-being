---
title: A birthday and three months later.
date: 2025-09-04
---

I *end up* writing the night of every birthday for as long as I could remember. Reading someone else’s essay made me stick to the tradition this year. 

*4th June*

My 22nd birthday ended a few minutes ago. Just a while ago, I was reading [Henrik Karlsson's 'On agency'](https://www.henrikkarlsson.xyz/p/agency), and found myself briefly caught in a moment of inspiration. I'm usually the kind of person who can be moved by a pigeon sitting on a hoarding. But to be inspired in a way that makes me take action has been rare. So I took the chance, after not having written anything for a year.

Birthdays are not my cup of tea. I do not enjoy the absurdity of being celebrated on the day I was born. What seems more worth celebrating is something that grew and changed several painstaking times to carve itself a place in the world. Maybe the absurdity comes from how strange it feels to treat a birthday as
something grand when it sits in the middle of fractured experiences of time we live through. Maybe I'm waiting for that one birthday where I feel like I want to own it, turn the tables and truly celebrate life. Until then, this is what the feeling is.

I went climbing with friends today. I didn't intend any symbolism when I chose it. It is simply an activity that is joyous enough to attempt and tiring enough to feel done. But life is seldom that simple when you have ever-dilated sight, ever-heightened sensitivities or when your mind's like a paper towel always getting drenched in water.

Our time at the climbing gym started quite well, all warmed and paced up. A few minutes into learning, we were left on our own to follow different routes ascending through levels. For the love of zeroes, this gym had an 'L0' sequence. Each level had roughly about three routes. The first two were a cakewalk while the third was an absolute menace. I was stuck towards the end, without an anchor to reach the end. One could choose to skip what didn't work and move to the upcoming levels, however, the idea of these levels being constructed for progressive difficulty was strongly rooted in me by then. I didn't want to go the 'wrong' way.
A few minutes later, I attempted the L1 routes. At L1, to my surprise, I finished many routes. But again I was stuck on one, falling three times at the same place. I was disappointed, and annoyed that I was the only one who couldn't move ahead while the rest did. I thought to myself, like all things in life, in the pursuit of one thing, I'm losing out on the rest. Particularly, health, friendships, good sleep and my social existence in general. I was wallowing in a feeling of losing vitality in life.

*2nd September*

A day after my birthday, one of my friends pointed out that it may not be that I wasn't strong enough to finish the route but it may have been that I didn't want to take a risk. It may not have been a limitation imposed by capability but the will to take a risk.
When I look at my life as a distant observer, I've rarely taken risks when they're not convenient. I've not risked anything when I've not been confident in my ability to accomplish a favourable outcome. Some might argue that those are not risks at all. I don't know if all risks need to have odds stacked against you. In retrospect, all of life looks like a gamble with hopes of having the odds unfold in our favour.

This is not how I imagined my birthday would go nor what I wanted. Somehow, it became symbolic of the routes I've been on so far. Over the course of three months after my birthday, I've been thinking about the times I had to make decisions that would compound and often ended up making decisions that simply had the most predictable outcome along with familiarity of context. I’ve taken on challenges only after having fully accepted that neither side of the outcomes can crumble me. I perhaps will not know what it means to have actually attempted to finish the route and taken a fall or move up the levels victoriously. 

I wonder if I’d visit the routes again *slightly changed and grown*. Would I choose to 'finish' the last leg of L0 or forcibly forget that it ever meant anything.
